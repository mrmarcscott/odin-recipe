Sweet Smoked Ribs

A sweet recipe for smoked pork ribs. I usually use baby back ribs but have had great success with spare ribs as well.

These pork ribs cook to perfection in your favorite smoker. Or simply add a teaspoon of liquid smoke into the rub and follow along using your oven.
Cook low and slow and you won't regret the beautiful aroma and flavors leaving your mouth watering.

A normal person would say this recipe is enough to feed a family of four. I say thats bull, you'll be telling your spouse and kids to find something else to eat.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup salt
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp packed brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp ground white pepper
  • 2 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp ground paprika
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 10 lbs baby back pork ribs
  • 1 cup apple juice
  • 1/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup bbq sauce

Directions

  1. Stir salt, white sugar, 2 tablespoons brown sugar, black pepper, white pepper, onion powder, garlic powder, chili powder, paprika, and cumin together in a small bowl to make the dry rub.
    Rub the spice mixture into the baby back ribs on all sides. Wrap the ribs well with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes prior to cooking.
  2. Unwrap baby back ribs and place onto the wire racks of the smoker in a single layer.
  3. Place the racks into a smoker, fill the smoker pan with apple, grape, pear, or cherry chips, and bring the smoker to 270 degrees F (130 degrees C). Smoke for 1 hour.
  4. Stir together the apple juice, 1/4 cup brown sugar, and the barbeque sauce. Brush the ribs with the sauce every 30 to 45 minutes after the first hour.
    Cook the ribs in the smoker until the meat is no longer pink and begins to "shrink" back from the bones, 3 to 4 hours. Brush the sauce onto the ribs one last time 30 minutes before the ribs are ready to be taken out of the smoker.
  5. Once the ribs are done, wrap them tightly with aluminum foil, and allow to rest 10 to 15 minutes. This will allow the juices to reabsorb into the meat and make the ribs moist.
